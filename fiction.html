About the book

In Memoriam

Winn's debut novel, In Memoriam, won the 2023 Waterstones Debut Fiction Prize. The novel is set during World War I and chronicles the love story between two soldiers. It is a vivid rendering of love and frontline brutality during the war. The novel is inspired by archive clippings from a student newspaper published between 1913-1919 from Winn's alma mater, Marlborough College. The protagonists, Gaunt and Ellwood, were inspired by readings of and about Robert Graves and Siegfried Sassoon, respectively. Winn's storytelling prowess is evident in her debut novel. In Memoriam is at once epic and intimate, humorous and profound. It captures the madness and legacy of the first world war as seen through the lens of a schoolboy love affair.

About the Author

Alice Winn

Alice Winn is an American novelist and screenwriter born in Paris, France in 1992 or 1993. She has dyslexia and did not learn to read until she was nine years old. Winn was educated at Marlborough College, followed by the University of Oxford, where she earned a degree in English literature. She currently resides in Brooklyn, New York City with her husband, Chris Turner, a British comedian, and their daughter.
